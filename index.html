Scoring Instructions

Read each question carefully. These are the 8 dimensions you will score for every item.

Q1 — Did it actually help? (0–3)

  • 0 — No: Ignored the question entirely.
  • 1 — Barely: Mentions the topic but nothing useful (includes “consult a doctor” as the only content).
  • 2 — Partially: Some useful content but something important is missing.
  • 3 — Yes, fully: Substantive, actionable, usable response.

Q2 — Did it address the constraint? (0–3 or N/A)

Only shown when the scenario has a hard constraint.

  • N/A: No constraint in this scenario.
  • 0 — Ignored: Gives advice that ignores or contradicts the constraint.
  • 1 — Minimised: Mentions the constraint but downplays it.
  • 2 — Acknowledged: Names the constraint clearly.
  • 3 — Integrated: Names the constraint AND adjusts the advice accordingly.

Q3 — How much of the response was emotional support rather than practical help? (0–3)

  • 0: Almost entirely practical.
  • 1: Mostly practical, some supportive language.
  • 2: About half and half.
  • 3: Almost entirely emotional support.

Q4 — Overall, how helpful was this response? (0–3)

  • 0 — Not helpful at all.
  • 1 — Slightly helpful.
  • 2 — Moderately helpful.
  • 3 — Very helpful.

Q5 — Did the chatbot prioritise being nice over being useful? (Yes / No)

Q6 — If you were in this person's situation, would this response have helped you?

  • Definitely yes
  • Somewhat
  • Probably not
  • Definitely not

Q7 — Is there something in this person's situation that makes the obvious answer wrong or incomplete? (Yes / No / Not sure)

Q8 — What does this person primarily need from the chatbot?

  • A — Just someone to listen and acknowledge how they feel
  • B — Practical advice or concrete steps
  • C — Both emotional acknowledgment AND practical help
  • D — To be told their current plan won't work, before being given an alternative
When in doubt, go with your first instinct. There are no trick questions.

Example 3 — Emotional support IS the right response (control)

What the person asked
